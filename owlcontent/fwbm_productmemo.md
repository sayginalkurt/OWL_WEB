## Product memo: Financial Well-Being Monitor (FWBM)

### 1. Executive summary

FWBM is positioned as a decision-grade financial well-being intelligence product for Türkiye. Its core offer is continuous, nationally representative household-level measurement of financial stress and resilience, delivered through weekly-updated dashboards, AI-assisted querying, monthly analytics, and integrated macro data. The product is explicitly designed for institutional users rather than consumers, with a stated target across policymakers, businesses, and civil society. ([fwbm.com.tr][1])

The product thesis is clear: conventional macro indicators such as inflation, growth, and employment are too aggregated and too lagging to explain how households are actually coping. FWBM positions itself as the “missing demand-side lens,” translating household behavior, pressure, expectations, and coping strategies into actionable intelligence. That framing gives the product a strong conceptual backbone and helps separate it from standard market trackers or one-off survey studies. ([fwbm.com.tr][2])

From a commercial standpoint, FWBM appears to sit in the category of research SaaS plus advisory intelligence. It is not presented merely as a dataset, nor merely as a consulting service. The public pages present it as an ongoing measurement infrastructure with a recurring delivery model, a score framework, a dashboard environment, and a narrative around intervention, targeting, and operational decision-making. ([fwbm.com.tr][1])

### 2. Core problem the product solves

FWBM is built around a simple gap in the market: there is abundant macroeconomic data, but insufficient continuous micro-level intelligence on how households experience economic strain in practice. The site argues that traditional indicators leave invisible the lived realities of households, including daily financial stress, shifting expectations, coping tactics, and resilience capacity. ([fwbm.com.tr][3])

That problem definition matters because it positions FWBM upstream of many institutional decisions. If a government wants to understand where hardship is intensifying, if a bank wants to anticipate financially stressed segments, or if a civil society actor wants to target interventions more precisely, macro aggregates alone are not enough. FWBM’s value proposition is that it makes this hidden layer measurable, visible, and operationally usable. ([fwbm.com.tr][4])

### 3. What the product is

Publicly, FWBM is described as a research-driven platform and a decision-grade research model. It measures households’ economic stress and resilience by combining survey-based household data with macroeconomic and international datasets, and then delivers that intelligence through dashboards, reports, and AI-enabled query tools. ([fwbm.com.tr][1])

In product terms, FWBM has four visible layers. First, it has a recurring data collection engine based on high-frequency CATI fieldwork. Second, it has a measurement framework that converts raw responses into a Financial Well-Being score structure. Third, it has a delivery layer consisting of dashboards, reporting outputs, and natural-language analysis. Fourth, it has an integration layer that connects FWBM data with external statistical sources such as OECD, World Bank, Eurostat, CBRT, TÜİK, and Google Data Commons. ([fwbm.com.tr][5])

### 4. Measurement and data architecture

The public site states that FWBM runs weekly intake and a rolling monthly sample of approximately 1,600 CATI interviews, with national coverage across 81 provinces and quotas by age, gender, and socio-economic or educational status. The homepage separately describes the product as nationwide and representative, with 1,600 interviews each month, a ±2.5% margin of error, and “100% quality control.” ([fwbm.com.tr][5])

The quality-control message is unusually prominent and functions as part of the product differentiation. FWBM says every interview is recorded and archived, and that controller re-contact is used to ensure authenticity and traceability from raw audio to final metric. This suggests that the platform is not only selling insight speed, but also selling trust in data provenance and field integrity. ([fwbm.com.tr][5])

The scoring framework is one of the most concrete product assets described on the site. FWBM says its score integrates 8 dimensions under 4 pillars: Income & Financial Capacity; Expenditure & Housing; Debt & Resilience; and Psycho-Social & Expectations. The dimensions include income stability, income–expenditure balance, essential versus non-essential spending, housing cost pressure, borrowing and repayment, savings and preparedness, expectations and mobility, and stress and support networks. ([fwbm.com.tr][5])

This matters strategically because the model goes beyond a narrow affordability or debt index. It blends financial capacity, behavior, preparedness, and subjective strain into a broader resilience construct. In market terms, that makes FWBM more defensible than a simple consumer confidence or household spending pulse, because it can speak to vulnerability, adaptation, and forward-looking pressure at the same time. ([fwbm.com.tr][5])

One point to note is that the homepage and the About FWBM page are not perfectly aligned in how they describe the architecture. The homepage references “up to 250 variables, 9 pillars, 5 index,” while the About FWBM page defines the score more specifically through 4 pillars and 8 dimensions. That does not necessarily indicate a contradiction, but it does indicate that the public product architecture is not yet fully standardized in how it is communicated. ([fwbm.com.tr][1])

### 5. Delivery model and user experience

The public offer is not limited to a static dashboard. FWBM presents an AI Analyst Assistant named Monet that supports natural-language queries and produces instant tables, charts, and maps. The site frames this as analytics at the speed of decision, which implies a low-friction interface for non-technical users to explore the data directly. ([fwbm.com.tr][5])

The delivery cadence is also explicit. FWBM lists weekly live dashboards, monthly analytic reports, quarterly public summaries, and expert podcasts. The homepage adds that the dashboard is updated weekly, supports rolling refresh, ad hoc cuts, and trend alerts, and is available 24/7. This is important because it positions FWBM as a subscription intelligence environment rather than a periodic research report. ([fwbm.com.tr][5])

The external integration layer strengthens the claim that FWBM is a decision environment rather than just a proprietary survey source. The site says the platform supports seamless joins with OECD SDMX, World Bank Data API, Eurostat, CBRT EVDS, TÜİK, and Google Data Commons, and also references an LLM-powered query layer, time-series tools for early-warning signals, entity harmonization, secure partner APIs, and embeddable dashboards. ([fwbm.com.tr][5])

### 6. Intended customers and use cases

The site clearly positions FWBM for three broad client groups: public sector institutions, private-sector organizations, and civil society or development actors. It does not present itself as a direct-to-consumer product. ([fwbm.com.tr][1])

For public institutions, the value proposition is better targeting, earlier detection of financial stress, and a stronger evidence base for inclusive policy and resilience planning. The site’s sector-playbook language frames financial well-being data as a measurable KPI for social protection, price-shock response, and inclusive growth strategy. ([fwbm.com.tr][4])

For the private sector, the site positions financial well-being as a risk and growth signal. It explicitly connects customer stress to delinquency, churn, lifetime value, segmentation, and unmet demand for products that help households smooth consumption and build buffers. This is an important commercial angle because it turns the product from a general social indicator into something tied to P&L, product design, and retention. ([fwbm.com.tr][4])

For civil society and development organizations, FWBM is framed as a tool for inclusion, targeting, climate-risk and shock response, and program design rooted in household realities rather than top-line macro indicators. The public messaging suggests that the product can support evidence-based intervention design in fragile or high-volatility environments. ([fwbm.com.tr][2])

### 7. Strategic positioning

FWBM’s strongest strategic move is that it occupies the space between official macro statistics and bespoke consulting research. It is not trying to outcompete official statistics on scale, nor compete with traditional survey firms on one-off execution alone. Instead, it is trying to own the category of continuous household financial well-being intelligence in Türkiye. ([fwbm.com.tr][1])

That position is strengthened by three things. First, the product has a clear intellectual rationale: macro indicators hide lived experience. Second, it has an operating model: weekly CATI plus rolling dashboard plus integrated external data. Third, it has a usable narrative for multiple budgets and buyers: policy targeting, customer insight, and social intervention. This multi-budget relevance increases its commercial optionality. ([fwbm.com.tr][2])

The team and advisory-board narrative also supports trust. The site says FWBM emerged from a partnership between Adhoc Research and SEAL, combining sociological and economic-quantitative capabilities. It also lists an advisory board including academics and a Research Manager from the Financial Health Network, which strengthens the perception of methodological seriousness and external credibility. ([fwbm.com.tr][3])

### 8. Commercial model visible from the site

Publicly, the commercial model appears to be subscription-led and sales-assisted. The site repeatedly refers to subscribing, includes a login environment, and promotes a free 10-day trial via contact. At the same time, it does not display public pricing, plan tiers, or a self-serve checkout flow. ([fwbm.com.tr][3])

This implies a relatively high-touch go-to-market model, likely built around demos, stakeholder education, and tailored packaging. That is consistent with the nature of the product, because institutional buyers generally need category explanation, use-case translation, and stakeholder buy-in before purchase. It also suggests that the commercial challenge is not only lead generation, but category creation and internal client enablement. ([fwbm.com.tr][3])

### 9. What is strong in the current public proposition

The product has a strong narrative coherence. The problem, product logic, and target sectors align well. The site does not read like a generic dashboard startup. It reads like a serious research-intelligence platform with a point of view about how economic reality should be measured. ([fwbm.com.tr][1])

The score architecture is also commercially useful because it is legible. Four pillars and eight dimensions are concrete enough to communicate, yet broad enough to cover multiple client use cases. That architecture gives the product a framework that can be reused across dashboards, reports, sales material, and investor narratives. ([fwbm.com.tr][5])

The weekly cadence, quality-control language, and external-data integration together make the product look operational rather than conceptual. Many research products stop at insight generation. FWBM’s public material suggests a workflow product: collect, validate, score, query, compare, and act. ([fwbm.com.tr][5])

### 10. Gaps or friction points in the public presentation

The main gap is product specificity. The site makes strong claims about capabilities, but it does not yet make the exact package structure fully explicit. A potential buyer can understand the concept, but still may not know precisely what is included in each subscription level, what the dashboard modules look like, which outputs are standard versus premium, or how different user roles would interact with the system. ([fwbm.com.tr][1])

A second issue is architecture clarity. As noted above, the homepage refers to “9 pillars” and “5 index,” while the main score description focuses on 4 pillars and 8 dimensions. For a product built on methodological credibility, consistency of taxonomy matters. Standardizing that language would improve trust and reduce friction in enterprise discussions. ([fwbm.com.tr][1])

A third issue is proof visibility. The site claims validation, reliability, and quality control, but the public pages do not yet surface enough technical proof artifacts for a skeptical institutional buyer, such as methodology notes, sample design sheets, indicator construction notes, example dashboards, benchmark outputs, or a clear explanation of what “scientifically validated” means in operational terms. ([fwbm.com.tr][1])

### 11. Bottom-line assessment

FWBM is a well-positioned household financial intelligence product with a credible market narrative and a potentially strong institutional fit. Its best public differentiator is that it does not merely measure economic sentiment; it measures household-level financial strain and resilience through a structured, repeated, and integrated system. That makes it materially more strategic than a generic pulse survey or macro commentary product. ([fwbm.com.tr][5])

In strategic language, FWBM appears to be building a new measurement layer for Türkiye: a micro-economic operating system for understanding how households experience the economy in real time. The public proposition is already serious and differentiated. The next maturity step would be sharper packaging, more visible proof assets, and tighter public articulation of the product architecture and customer workflow. ([fwbm.com.tr][1])
