Beyza Polat, Ph.D. CO-Founder / Managing Partner

Beyza Polat is the Founding Partner and Managing Director of SEAL Consulting. She is an applied microeconomist with an expertise on the analysis of large-scale datasets and development and implementation of quantitative research tools to contribute to the decision-making process of public and private enterprises. Since 2014, she has been working as a team leader and/or a consultant in projects with the Ministry of Industry and Technology, Ministry of Trade, The World Bank, United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees, Unites Nations Development Program, Turkish Employment Agency and SAS Institute.

She has experience in quantitative impact evaluation, program evaluation, regional economic analysis, labor market assessments and sectoral economic analysis. She also gives executive training to the public officials and the private sector on data analytics and impact evaluation. She previously worked as an Assistant Professor of Economics at Özyeğin University where she taught Statistics, Econometrics and Applied Microeconometrics courses. She also taught undergraduate and graduate level courses at Bilkent University and LSE. Beyza holds an undergraduate degree in Business Administration from Bilkent University and graduate degrees in Economics from Bilkent University and the London School of Economics and Political Science.



Saygın Vedat Alkurt CO-Founder / Managing Partner

Saygın Vedat Alkurt holds a Bachelor’s and a Master’s degree in Sociology from Middle East Technical University (ODTÜ), Ankara. With over 15+ years of interdisciplinary research experience, he has contributed to and led numerous academic, public, and civil society projects across Turkey, particularly in fields such as political sociology, communication, youth studies, and strategic consultancy. He currently serves as the General Director of Adhoc Research and Consultancy, which he co-founded, and as the Chairperson of CORE Institute (Collective Research Association), an NGO focusing on generational change, urban mobility, and socio-cultural transformation.

He is also a lecturer at Kadir Has University’s Faculty of Communication and sits on the Advisory Board of the Department of Political Science and Public Administration at the same university.

Alkurt’s portfolio spans research design, qualitative and quantitative analysis, monitoring and evaluation, and data-driven policy consulting for international organizations such as UNDP, European Union institutions, and TUBITAK. He is known for bridging data and narrative, combining robust field methodologies with participatory approaches to produce actionable insights for social impact. Throughout his career, he has maintained a strong focus on public interest research and the intersection of societal behavior, governance, and policy.
